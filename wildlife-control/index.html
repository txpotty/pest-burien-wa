Common Problems and Signs You Need Wildlife Control

Recognizing the signs of a wildlife problem early on can prevent significant damage and stress. Many issues can indicate that you have unwanted wildlife visitors. Being aware of these signs can help you take swift action to protect your property and your family's health and safety.

Strange Noises

Are you hearing scratching, thumping, or scurrying sounds coming from your attic, walls, or crawl space? These noises are often a strong indicator of wildlife activity. Nocturnal animals like raccoons, squirrels, or even mice can be quite active at night, leading to disturbances that disrupt your sleep and peace of mind. Ignoring these sounds could mean ignoring a growing problem that could lead to costly repairs down the line. Our expert team can help identify the source of these sounds and implement effective removal strategies.

Visible Damage

Wildlife can cause a variety of visible damage to your property. This might include chewed wires, damaged insulation, ripped screens, torn vents, or even structural damage to roofing or siding. Squirrels, for instance, are notorious for gnawing on various materials, creating fire hazards in some cases if they damage electrical wiring. Opossums and raccoons can tear up siding or attic ventilation to gain entry. Inspecting your property regularly for these signs of damage is a good preventative measure. If you spot such damage, it's a strong signal that it's time to call in professionals for wildlife control services in Burien, WA.

Unpleasant Odors

Wildlife can leave behind unpleasant odors from their droppings, urine, or nesting materials. These smells can permeate your home and indicate an infestation. Skunks are perhaps the most obvious culprits due to their pungent spray, but other animals also leave tell-tale scents. These odors are not only bothersome but can also pose health risks due to potential pathogens in animal waste. Professional cleanup and sanitization are often necessary to eliminate these odors and associated health hazards. Burien Pest Pros has the tools and expertise to address these odor issues effectively.

Fouled Garbage Cans

Do you find your garbage cans repeatedly tipped over or raided? This is a classic sign of scavenging wildlife like raccoons or opossums. These animals are attracted to the easy food source your trash provides. Securing your garbage cans with animal-proof lids or storing them in a secure location can help deter these nocturnal visitors, but ongoing problems may require professional intervention to remove the animals and identify alternative food sources attracting them to your property.

Animal Droppings

Finding animal feces in or around your home is a clear indicator of a wildlife presence. The appearance and location of the droppings can often help identify the type of animal. Animal waste can carry diseases and parasites, making proper cleanup and sanitation essential. It's important to avoid direct contact with animal droppings and call in professionals for safe removal and disinfection. Our team is trained in the safe handling and disposal of animal waste.

Nesting Materials

Discovering nesting materials in unusual places like your attic, eaves, or shed is a strong sign that wildlife is making themselves at home. Animals use a variety of materials, such as leaves, twigs, insulation, or shredded paper, to build their nests. The presence of nests indicates an ongoing infestation and a potential breeding site. Removing nests and sealing off entry points are critical steps in resolving the issue. We can help you locate and remove these nests safely and prevent future nesting activities.

Visible Animals

Seeing animals on your property during the day or night can be a clear sign of an issue. While some wildlife sightings are normal, frequent sightings, especially of animals acting aggressively or appearing sick, warrant concern. If you see animals entering or exiting your home through specific points, it's crucial to take action immediately. Do not attempt to handle wildlife yourself, as they can be unpredictable and potentially dangerous. Always contact a professional for safe and humane removal.
